This is a technical drawing of my Thesis Project: 2-axis Cartesian robotic system with A* pathfinding for automated medication dispensing in smart Pharmacy Logistics (design & assemble by me)

The project focuses on the research and development of an automated pharmaceutical dispensing system for pharmacies, aiming to optimize medication management and dispensing workflows, minimize human error, and enhance service efficiency. The system integrates Smart Logistics (LogTech) technologies, including automated storage, barcode/RFID-based drug identification and retrieval, anti-fraud control, route optimization, and internal order coordination.

In addition, the project incorporates mechatronic modules to execute medication dispensing based on prescriptions entered into the system. A user interface (UI) will also be developed to enable interaction with the system and to manage medication data, including inventory levels, expiration dates, and batch information. The proposed application is intended to contribute to the development of smart pharmacies by supporting inventory control, traceability, and accurate, transparent pharmaceutical distribution.

More about Thesis

The LY-01 DC 12–24V solenoid lock is utilized as an actuating mechanism for the latch system in the medicine dispensing module. During the dispensing process, the latch bar temporarily retains the medication inside the pickup mechanism, preventing unintended release caused by the inclined geometry of the storage chute. Once the mechanism reaches the designated dispensing position, the solenoid retracts the latch, allowing the medication to drop accurately into the doctor’s receiving compartment.

The 2020 aluminum V-slot linear motion kit, equipped with large V-wheels, is used to facilitate smooth and stable Y-axis motion of the medicine-picking mechanism along the 2020 V-slot aluminum profile.

Link 1 and Link 2 are symmetrically arranged components within the four-bar linkage mechanism.

The lifting mechanism is based on a symmetrical four-bar parallelogram linkage connected through 693ZZ Bearing bearings and secured using M3 bolts and nuts. The mechanism operates according to Grashof’s Law: s+l=p+q. This configuration enables stable vertical motion during the medicine retrieval process. The mechanism is actuated by a Tower Pro MG996R Servo Motor servo motor, which rotates to lift the medicine container from the tray and securely retain it within the retrieval frame for transportation to the dispensing area.

The Tower Pro MG996R 360° servo motor is used to rotate the medicine-picking mechanism.
